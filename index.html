For more than a decade, the Gibbs Land Use and Environment (GLUE) Lab has been advancing research and solutions to address the planet’s most pressing issues related to deforestation, grassland conversion, sustainable agriculture, public health, and climate change. We publish in top journals and work closely with policymakers, companies, civil society, and other researchers to lead Land System Science that matters to the world. Human land-use change has transformed our world more than any other environmental change. These land-use impacts rival those from climate change and pressure continues to mount with growing population and consumption trends. In GLUE, we study human environment interactions and aim to reconcile conservation, climate change, and food security by informing policy and market-based strategies. Our work spans the globe, but with emphasis in Brazil, Colombia and the United States. We use GIS, remotely sensed imagery, econometrics, Big Data, and commodity supply-chain analysis combined with stakeholder interviews to understand how and why humans use land around the world, and quantify what these changes mean for ecosystem services, particularly carbon storage and emissions. GLUE began in 2011 when Holly Gibbs began her faculty position at UW but has since evolved into a diverse, multi-disciplinary research team co-led by lead scientists, Tyler Lark and Lisa Rausch, along with staff researchers and data engineers. We strive for work-life balance and a strong sense of joy and community in our lab. We are housed in the Center for Sustainability and the Global Environment (SAGE), which is a multidisciplinary research center in the Nelson Institute for Environmental Studies at UW-Madison with about fifty faculty, scientists, students, and staff examining cutting-edge environmental challenges and solutions.  Students in GLUE earn degrees with the Nelson Institute, the Department of Geography, and the Department of Applied and Agricultural Economics.
